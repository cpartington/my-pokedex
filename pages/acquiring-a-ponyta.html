Acquiring a Ponyta, plus some more

After desperately searching for a Pokémon Shield player who could trade me a Ponyta, I resigned myself to the depressing fact that I’d never get one. I almost regretted choosing Sword over Shield—while Zacian is certainly a cuter legendary than Zamazenta, Ponyta exists exclusively in the Shield game, leaving me with an ugly fighting bird.

I thought briefly about buying Pokémon Shield, but that would be a frivolous use of my money. I considered begging random people on the Internet to trade with me, but I was too distrustful to follow through. After hours of anxious ruminating, I decided to give up. Maybe I’d just never see one.

And then, one day, everything changed. One of my good friends mentioned that his brother had Pokémon Shield and then arranged for his brother to stop by our apartment. I was ecstatic. When the moment of truth arrived, we traded several pokémon and I finally had a Ponyta.

Even more exciting was that his brother set his language to French, resulting in the pokémon I recieved from him being foreign pokémon. Apparently this has many benefits, including better breeding and better chances of shiny pokémon. If I ever chose to walk down the shiny road, I’d have slightly less terrible odds.
