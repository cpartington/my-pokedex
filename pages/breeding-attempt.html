A failed breeding attempt

With a great-quality Dragapult and a great-quality Ditto, I was ready to breed a perfect Dreepy. I had high hopes for success since my Ditto was also foreign. I thought I could get an amazing-quality Dreepy in maybe a couple tries.

Well, I was quickly disappointed. My first egg hatched into a Dreepy that was good, and the next two were only okay. What was going on? Where was the magic? How did people do this for a hundred eggs? Heck, some people did it for a thousand or more. I certaintly don’t have the patience for that.

Very quickly, my motivation died. Looks like I wasn’t ready to walk down the long and tedious road after all. I decided to just level up the Dragapult I already had. The big question remaining now was what made this pokémon so great in the first place. It fainted in a single hit from one of Leon’s pokémon and didn’t seem to be particularly strong.

My brother said I should stick to the stack that he recommended in the beginning. Maybe he was right.
